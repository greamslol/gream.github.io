General Rules
Remaining In Character (Type 0 - Type 1)
PGN Classic strives to be an immersive roleplay and it is important that players remain in
character (IC) both in game and within designated IC Discord Channels. Roleplay should be
uninterrupted and players are expected to handle all situations presented to them in character.
In Game Expectations
- Players should never break character while in game. This includes discussing server
mechanics, rule disputes, or real life topics in voice, chat or actions.
- If a rule break occurs, players are expected to continue to roleplay the situation and
report the issue afterwards through our report systems. Players should never pause,
disrupt or derail the scene to address the violation in game.
Discord Expectations
- Certain channels within PGN Official Discords are designated for in-character
discussions, communications and roleplay interactions. These channels are to remain
strictly in character and may not contain any OOC comments or real life discussions
- This only applies to discords officially owned by PGN. Gangs and business
discords should always be considered OOC
- Anything roleplayed in game must stay in character and should not be discussed OOC.
This includes using OOC chats or dms to influence and IC situation
- Information from IC interactions should never be used in OOC discussions and vice
versa. Avoid using discord to arrange or influence RP scenes in an unfair manner.

Making Reports to Classic Command
Ensuring a fair and well-managed RP experience requires proper reporting procedures. All
reports should be submitted professionally, with clear details and supporting evidence to help
Classic Command address issues effectively
Submitting a Report
All reports must be submitted through the in game report system or a Classic Command ticket
system. Ensure that your ticket includes:
● Name/ID of the individual being reported.
● A detailed reason outlining the rule(s) broken.
● Your Discord username for follow-up communication.
● Relevant server details (date, time, and location of the incident).
Do NOT:
● Report minor RP disagreements that could be handled IC.
● Spam or submit multiple reports for the same issue.
● Use reports for personal conflicts or revenge.
Providing Evidence
● A strong report must include verifiable evidence to support your claims. Please ensure:
● Video evidence is uploaded via Medal, Google Drive, or a similar platform.
● Files are stored and backed up—online links may expire or be deleted.
● Screenshots (if applicable) are clear and unedited.
Reports without sufficient evidence may not be actionable.
False Report (Type 4)
Submitting false, misleading, or exaggerated reports is strictly prohibited and will be taken
seriously. Reports should be used to address genuine rule violations, not as a tool for
harassment, revenge, or wasting staff time.

Consequences for False Reporting
Making a false or misleading report may result in:
● Immediate disciplinary action (warning or restriction from filing future reports).
● Suspension or removal from the community, depending on severity.
● Escalating punishments for repeated offenses, including permanent bans.
● Any attempt to fabricate evidence, manipulate facts, or knowingly accuse someone
falsely will result in severe consequences.
Reporting Responsibly
To ensure fairness, all reports must be truthful, accurate, and supported by evidence. Before
filing a report:
● Be honest—never exaggerate or manipulate details.
● Provide valid evidence (video clips, chat logs, or screenshots).
● Ask for clarification if you are unsure whether something is reportable.
● If you suspect someone is breaking a rule but lack proof, do NOT make baseless
accusations. Instead, gather evidence before reporting.

Powergaming (Type 2 - Type 3)
Powergaming is the act of using unrealistic or unfair mechanics to gain an advantage in
roleplay, negatively affecting other players' experiences. All characters must have realistic
limitations and weaknesses, ensuring balanced and immersive roleplay.
What is Powergaming?
Powergaming occurs when a player:
● Forces an outcome that others cannot realistically counter.
● Ignores logical character limitations.

4.0 Classic Server Rules
● Uses game mechanics in a way that breaks immersion or creates an unfair advantage.
Examples of Powergaming
The following actions are considered powergaming and are not allowed:
● Using God Mode, Super Heavy Armor, or other unfair defensive advantages.
● Healing or repairing vehicles without proper roleplay.
● Unrealistically recovering from severe injuries (e.g., getting shot multiple times and
immediately running away).
● Escaping restraints, cuffs, or prison without proper RP or permission.
● Spawning items, weapons, or money your character did not previously obtain through
RP.
● Using third-party modifications to enhance abilities (e.g., blips, seeing player names,
speed hacks).
● Creating an all-knowing, all-powerful character who has unrealistic skills in every
situation.
● Ignoring physical limitations (e.g., infinite sprinting, holding breath indefinitely, no
fatigue from physical strain).
● Making vague, unrealistic excuses (e.g., “I’m built different” or “I took special drugs”)
without any meaningful in-character consequences.
Metagaming (Type 2)
Metagaming is the act of using out-of-character (OOC) information to influence in-character
(IC) decisions, giving a player an unfair advantage and disrupting immersive roleplay. This rule
ensures that all actions taken in RP are based solely on IC knowledge. Metagaming occurs
when a player acts on information their character wouldn’t realistically know. This unfairly
impacts roleplay and disrupts fair play.
Examples of Metagaming?
The following are examples of metagaming and are not allowed:
● Using Discord, DMs, or OOC chat to gain RP information your character has not learned
in-game.

● Starting unnecessary or unrealistic pursuits just for fun.
Exception: If a character has a genuine IC reason to engage with law enforcement (e.g., being
part of a gang, committing a crime), the interaction must still be realistic and not excessively
disruptive.
Passive / Priority RP
PGN roleplay is structured into Passive RP and Priority RP, each serving a distinct purpose in
creating engaging and immersive storytelling. Players should balance both types to ensure
realistic, fair, and enjoyable RP for everyone.
Priority Roleplay
Priority RP refers to scenarios that require significant attention and emergency response. These
are classified into Large Priority RP and Small Priority RP based on scale, threat level, and
response required.

Large Priority RP
Definition: Scenarios requiring major emergency response and at least three units. These
events are high-risk and demand well-planned execution.
Examples of Large Priority RP:
● Vehicle Pursuits (10-80s)
● Bank Robberies
● Hostage Situations
● Major Accidents (Multi-Car Collisions, Explosions, etc.)

● Medical Calls for Minor Injuries
● Foot Pursuits for Non-Violent Offenders
● Domestic Disturbances (Verbal Arguments, Noise Complaints, etc.)

● Maintain realistic context. Avoid priorities that feel forced or senseless.

Passive Roleplay
Passive RP includes non-emergency interactions that focus on character development,
world-building, and social engagement. Passive RP forms the foundation for compelling
storytelling and future priorities.
Examples of Passive RP:
● Working or Visiting Businesses (Restaurants, Mechanics, Nightclubs, etc.)
● Camping, Fishing, or Road Trips
● Social Events, Gatherings, and Meetups
● Responding to IC Advertisements
● Casual Character Interactions & Conversations
Why Passive RP Matters
● Strengthens Character Development – Every great story starts with meaningful daily
interactions.
● Encourages Creativity – Allows players to explore unique storylines and personal arcs.
● Builds Meaningful Storylines – Passive RP creates the context for future priority
scenarios.
● Fosters Friendships & Community Growth – Engaging in passive RP helps players
connect IC and OOC.
● Earns Positive Recognition – Strong passive RP can lead to commendations and
promotions.
● Elevates Overall RP Quality – A server with well-developed passive RP ensures more
immersive priority scenes.
Final Note:
Passive RP should be prioritized as the core of roleplay. Priority RP should be intentional,
character-driven, and not overused.

Realistic Reactions (Type 1 - Type 2)
Roleplay should always prioritize realism, ensuring that injuries, fear response and
character survival instincts are roleplayed authentically. Your character is not invincible
and their actions should reflect the real-life consequences of dangerous situations.
InjuryRP
Your character must acknowledge all injuries they would reasonably sustain and must
roleplay the effects of their injuries. Minor Injuries may allow you to continue normal
activity with some discomfort while serious injuries should incapacitate your character
until they receive medical attention potentially resulting in long term or life altering
consequences.Treatment of such injuries should not have instant recovery but the
rehabilitation can also be roleplayed. If you RP your injury is Fatal, your character will
be considered permanently dead.
FearRP
FearRP means your character should act as a real person would in life threatening or
high stress situations. You are not a superhero, a robot, a fearless robot, or immune to
fear and must react to all threats in a realistic manner. This means no unreasonable
heroics, your character should not attempt to pull a gun, run, or attack someone if doing
so is likely to get you killed. Trying to fight off multiple armed captors alone is not
realistic. Civilians should also respect the authority and danger of LEO interactions.
Attacking officers with no proper reason or running from the police over minor charges
is not realistic
Value of Life
Characters should have a survival instinct and value their own life, resulting in
reasonable, rational and realistic choices when faced with danger. Characters should
not act in a reckless manner that may put their life in danger without a good reason, nor
should your character willingly put themselves into a high stakes situation unless they
have solid justification to do so
